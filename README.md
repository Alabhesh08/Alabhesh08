<h1>Hi there, I'm Labhesh Sonawane 👋</h1>

Biomedical Engineering graduate student at <b>IIT Madras</b> working at the intersection of <b>biomechanics, biomedical signal processing, embedded systems, and AI for healthcare.</b>

My work primarily involves developing hardware–software systems for physiological signal acquisition, wearable sensing, human motion analysis, and biomedical instrumentation. Recently, I’ve also started exploring deep learning and medical imaging workflows for healthcare applications.

I enjoy building interdisciplinary systems that combine electronics, physiology, signal processing, and intelligent computation.

## Selected Projects

- ### HECKTOR – Multimodal Tumor Segmentation
  Worked on multiclass segmentation of head & neck tumors using PET/CT imaging and explored deep learning pipelines for medical image analysis.

- ### Gait Phase Detection using EMG & Motion Tracking
  Developed a real-time gait phase detection pipeline using lower-limb EMG and motion tracking during internship at DEBEL, DRDO.

- ### Wireless Whole-Body Kinematics Measurement System
  Built an IMU-based human motion tracking platform involving embedded systems, sensor synchronization, and motion acquisition.

- ### Heart Rate Variability Analysis
  Worked on ECG preprocessing, HRV feature extraction, and robust QRS detection under noisy physiological conditions.
